Jordan Krueger

UI Design. Web Design. Fine Art. Photography. Writing.

Welcome to my portfolio site. In addition to my curated projects below, you can check for more recent examples, works in progress, and other rants and musings in the navigation above.

  1. This project demonstrates my use of color and space when arranging and creating icons. My goal was to keep intact the color schemes used throughout the site, particularly with previous graphics used.

    Icon Arrangement and Color Schemes

    Role: Graphic Designer
    Date: June 2013
  2. In this project, I turned a lackluster and intuitive design into a polished, well-thought-out interface. I kept the layout and type simple and usable while adding some nuance here and there with small amounts of dropshadow and shading to make elements like headers and buttons stand out. I also created the background for the site and aided in the design of the logo.

    Redesign of AccountingDegree.com

    Role: User Interface Designer
    Date: December 2012
  3. I did some work on a medical billing and coding site. In addition to refining the article design and stylizing the monetization for the site, I designed this job board. Unfortunately it has not yet been implemented, but the mock-ups give good sense of how it would work. As you can see, I placed hover states next to some of the inactive buttons.

    Job Board Design

    Role: User Interface Designer
    Date: February 2013
  4. I was given images and content that corresponded to each slide. The project was a very open-ended, "design a slideshow." I decided to model the design based on the appearance of a timeline, since the slideshow content was very time-focused ("Today's University Is" and "Tomorrow's University Will Be").

    Dynamic Slideshow on a Timeline

    Role: User Interface Designer
    Date: March 2012
  5. For this project, I redesigned a finance website and its logo. One unique challenge to this project was that the site had a lot of repeat traffic, so my goal was to make the redesign look as good as possible without disrupting the experience of long-time users. Most of the changes I made were typography and white space. I also created pull-quotes and a Q&A format to give the articles a sharper, more polished look.

    Site and Logo Redesign

    Role: User Interface Designer
    Date: January 2013
